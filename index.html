About Me

I embarked on my undergraduate studies at the College of Life Science of Wuhan University with a focus on biology science, performing well in basic courses learning. I took part in the “First-Grade Program” organized by the Wuhan Institute of Virology, Chinese Academy of Sciences and joined Gongpeng Lab in October, 2018. During this year, I have done some surveys on Glms Ribozyme and RdRP. I successfully complete the problem that improve the self-cleave efficiency of Glms Ribozyme, using methods like site-specific mutagenesis, RNA structure prediction and analysis. I finally got the first prize in the oral defense. After this scientific research training, I have a better knowledge about structural biology and systems biology, improve my experimental skills and put forward some interesting questions such as the evolutionary meaning of the unstable P2.2 domain of Glms Ribozyme, keeping exploring now. I participated in 2019 UBC VSP program, including Course A “Molecular Mechanism of Diseases” and Course B “Biochemistry and Society: Current Issues”, with A+ (90) and A (88) respectively in the final exam. It provides me with a opportunity to experience the education pattern of North America and made up my mind to head for North America for summer internship and Ph.D.

Although I am just a sophomore, I have developed my strong interest in natural sciences and determined to bend myself to fundamental research. I yearn for high stages to keep exploring and relishing the beauty of biological science. Hence, I sincerely hope you, an esteemed professor, can offer me this precious opportunity.
